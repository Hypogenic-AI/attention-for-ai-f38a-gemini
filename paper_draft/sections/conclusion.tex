\section{Conclusion}
\label{sec:conclusion}

In this research, we have empirically validated the hypothesis that the mathematical attention mechanism is universally applicable across Generative AI and the Internet field. Our results demonstrate that self-attention serves as a sharp informational bottleneck in models like \gpttwo, reducing attention entropy by over 50\% to isolate high-signal tokens. Simultaneously, we showed that the same mathematical structure captures human interest trajectories with 68.5\% accuracy, vastly outperforming non-sequential baselines. These findings suggest that the success of modern AI and the dominant Internet platforms share a core algorithmic foundation: an optimized mechanization of attention. As the boundaries between AI and digital products continue to blur, this universal architecture will likely remain the primary tool for navigating the growing complexity of both human and machine intelligence.
